Game-Based Learning for Cybersecurity Education

Imagine a cybersecurity class where students don’t just listen—they act, solve, and play their way through real-world scenarios. This is the core idea behind game-based learning (GBL) for cybersecurity education. It shifts the learning experience from theoretical lectures to immersive, interactive environments, combining elements of challenge, engagement, and experimentation.

By leveraging platforms like Hack The Box and TryHackMe, students gain access to safe, ethical hacking simulations. These platforms create a sandbox where aspiring cybersecurity professionals learn through failure and experimentation, crucial in real-life attack mitigation.

Additionally, incorporating Linux command-line labs and network traffic analyzers like Wireshark sharpens student’s hands-on skills. They not only learn concepts but also master tools used in the industry.

A study by the Journal of Cybersecurity Education, Research, and Practice (2022) highlighted that learners retained up to 60% more knowledge when cybersecurity concepts were taught using gamified techniques versus traditional lectures.

In my own teaching, I integrate Raspberry Pi devices to simulate small-scale networks, tasking students with defending these against mock cyber-attacks. This approach builds real-world readiness and a deeper sense of problem-solving.

Game-based learning goes beyond technical skills—it nurtures resilience, strategic thinking, and teamwork, all critical attributes in the cybersecurity field today.
